Identity, Values and Strengths

During my studies (BFA in Fashion and Sustainability) I often considered my ethics and values, as the fashion industry is so rife with exploitation of both people and resources. This forced me to confront my place and the implications of my contributions to the sector.
As part of my end of year collection, I chose to employ different sustainable practices to reflect my personal values and to support ideas presented in my Fashion Theory paper. Mainly my focus was to make my garments bio-degradable by constructing them with exclusively natural fibres, not using any notions (metal buttons, zips etc.) and natural-dyeing, using plant extracts. I already had all of the fabrics and plant extracts needed for my collection when we went into a lockdown but because of covid restrictions sourcing the silk thread that I'd intended on using became increasingly difficult. Most threads available are a polyester/blend meaning they're non-biodegradable and while this is a minor detail, I felt that using the polyester thread immediately available to me undermined the integrity of my work.
Because of time-restraints it was a decision between guaranteeing I finish my collection and get my degree, or wait to source silk thread and risk breaching my deadline. I chose to continue with polyester thread in order to finish my collection because ultimately that was more important to me but I was really disappointed that I ended up producing something that contradicted the values that informed my planning and research.

Compassion, connection and integrity are three of my core values.

A strength that aids my learning is my creativity. For a long time I viewed my creativity as something that was purely practical, based on my love of drawing and making things with my hands. I grew to realise that creativity is expressed in many ways, at times beneficial and others limiting. It drives me to find solutions, allowing me to approach challenges from a nuanced perspective but it also manifests in less productive ways such as daydreaming. I often find my mind wandering, which leads to one of my biggest limitations in terms of my learning and development - my struggle to pay attention or maintain focus. This plays into my tendency to procrastinate, sometimes I'll avoid tasks in response to the frustration I feel about how difficult it can be for me to buckle down and get things done.

An example of when I experienced resistance/tension when trying to work productively with others occurred during my first year at University. We had been assigned into small groups of around 5 students and our assignment was to make a short film. Because we were on a short time-frame, the work involved a lot of out-of-school hours. It became clear very quickly that people had differing amounts of investment into the project but the work involved the participation of all students. The student who had been assigned the leader of the project, put a lot of pressure on themselves to achieve high results but was struggling with the weight of responsibility.
The challenge for me was trying to make sure everyone was happy, so that we could work together effectively. I consoled the team leader, encouraging breaks in order to calm themselves. From there we were able to come up with a plan and designated roles to each member of the team, so that responsibilities were more fairly distributed.
In the future I would encourage better planning and distribution of responsibilities from the beginning of a project. I think listing goals and expectations is really helpful in group-work situations, as it allows you to create a shared vision. This isn't possible without open discussion and inclusivity.

Emotional Intelligence

What is emotional intelligence?
Emotional intelligence refer to a person's ability to identify, perceive, control and express emotions.

"The ability to monitor one's own and others feelings and emotions, to discriminate among them and to use this information to guide one's thinking and actions.‚Äù
- Salovey and Mayer 1990

How is it different to IQ?
Intelligence Quotient (IQ) is a measure of a persons numerical and analytical intelligence, referring to their ability to use logic and reason to problem solve.
Emotional Quotient (EQ) refers to a person's emotional intelligence, meaning their self-awareness, self regulation, motivation, empathy and social skills.
Unlike IQ, EQ is not derived from a set of standardised tests.

Why is it important?
Having good emotional intelligence benefits many aspects of life, as it dictates the relationships we have with ourselves and others.

Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

Neuroplasticity
Being aware of the the principles of neuroplasticity improves both morale and performance. By understanding that intelligence and ability aren't predetermined, we are able to use that knowledge to inform the way we engage with new information, content and practices. Repetition is one way to increase neuroplasticity, practice helps you build stronger connections in the brain but retrieving memories and reviewing material and experience. Another useful tool is to put information into context. Introducing new information by relating it to existing knowledge allows you to build on existing pathways form stronger connections.
Watch this Introduction to Neuroplasticity

Growth Mindset
Growth mindset is the counter to fixed mindset, it describes ones attitude towards their learning ability. In a fixed mindset you may believe that your abilities, intelligence and talents are set traits; resistant to change or improvement. In a growth mindset you understand that your abilities, intelligence and talents can be developed and learned with effort, persistence and good teaching. I think in conjunction with awareness of neuroplasticity, adopting a growth mindset is super empowering. It encourages me to be persistent, which is especially important when challenges seem too big or overwhelming.
Watch Carol Dweck's talk on Growth Mindset

Neuroplasticity, Growth Mindset and My Learning Plan
These understandings of neuroplasticity and growth mindset as concepts shape and influence the way I will approach my learning plan and strategy. One of the challenges outlined in my learning plan is the difficulties I face with time-management. I think this is due to procrastination as a response to low self-confidence when in working abilities. With these two concepts in mind I will be able to face challenges more easily, knowing that failure is really a result of not trying.
